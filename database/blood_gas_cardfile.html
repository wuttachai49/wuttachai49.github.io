Compensation Degree

Uncompensated — No corrective response; pH remains outside normal range
Partially Compensated — Compensating parameter changes, pH approaches but does not reach 7.35–7.45
Completely Compensated — pH restored to normal range (7.35–7.45); both HCO₃⁻ and PaCO₂ remain abnormal

3-Step Interpretation Method
1
Check pH
pH < 7.35 → Acidosis pH > 7.45 → Alkalosis pH 7.35–7.45 → Normal
2
Identify Primary Cause
Look at HCO₃⁻ and PaCO₂ — which one is abnormal in the same direction as pH change?
HCO₃⁻ abnormal → Metabolic PaCO₂ abnormal → Respiratory
3
Assess Compensation
Is the remaining parameter changing in the opposite direction?
pH still outside 7.35–7.45 AND compensating param normal → Uncompensated
pH outside range AND compensating param also abnormal → Partially Compensated
pH within 7.35–7.45 AND both params abnormal → Completely Compensated
