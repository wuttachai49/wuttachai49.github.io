Plot 1: NORMAL LYMPHOCYTE SUBPOPULATION (T-Cell Subset)

Clinical Context: Healthy adult with normal immune function

Plot A: FSC-SSC (Lymphocyte Gate)
Plot B: CD3 vs CD45 (T-Cell Population)
Plot C: CD4 vs CD8 (T-Cell Subsets)
Plot D: CD3 Histogram (Intensity)

✓ NORMAL INTERPRETATION

Key Findings:

  • FSC-SSC: Clear lymphocyte gate (red cluster) separated from monocytes and granulocytes
  • CD3 Expression: 75% of gated lymphocytes are CD3+ (T cells)
  • CD4:CD8 Ratio: 2:1 (40% ÷ 20%) — Normal is 1.5-3.0
  • All populations: CD45 positive—confirms leukocytes

Absolute Counts (example): Total lymphocytes 1500/μL → CD4: 600/μL (40%), CD8: 300/μL (20%)

Clinical Status: Normal immune function. No immunodeficiency detected.

Reference Ranges for Normal Adults:
  • CD3+ (T cells): 65-85% or 800-2200 cells/μL
  • CD4+ (Helper T cells): 35-50% or 400-1200 cells/μL
  • CD8+ (Killer T cells): 15-30% or 150-900 cells/μL
  • CD4:CD8 Ratio: 1.5-3.0

Plot 2: ABNORMAL - HIV PATIENT WITH LOW CD4 COUNT

Clinical Context: Patient with untreated HIV infection and severe immunosuppression (CD4 = 85 cells/μL)

T-Cell Subset (CD4 vs CD8)
CD3 Histogram (Depleted T Cells)

⚠️ ABNORMAL - IMMEDIATE ACTION REQUIRED

Key Findings:

  • CD3+: 45% (VERY LOW; normal 65-85%)
  • CD4+: 2% absolute (85 cells/μL) — CRITICAL; severe immunodeficiency
  • CD8+: 60% (elevated; normal 15-30%)
  • CD4:CD8 Ratio: 0.03 (SEVERELY INVERTED; normal 1.5-3.0)
  • Abnormal double-negative CD4-CD8- population visible in Q3

Clinical Action: STAT physician notification. Patient at extreme risk for:

  • Pneumocystis pneumonia (PCP)
  • Cytomegalovirus (CMV) infections
  • Mycobacterium avium complex (MAC)
  • Opportunistic infections

Recommendation: Urgent initiation/optimization of antiretroviral therapy (ART)

Plot 3: NORMAL HLA-B27 - NEGATIVE RESULT

Clinical Context: Patient without ankylosing spondylitis symptoms

Isotype Control (Background Staining)
HLA-B27 Test (Negative)

✓ NEGATIVE HLA-B27 RESULT

Key Findings:

  • Isotype Control: Minimal background (<5% in upper-right quadrant)
  • HLA-B27 Test: Similar or slightly lower staining than isotype
  • MFI (Mean Fluorescence Intensity): <4860
  • Cells in HLA-B27+ gate (Q4): <5%

Clinical Interpretation: NEGATIVE — HLA-B27 ABSENT

Clinical Significance:

  • Ankylosing spondylitis is effectively ruled out (~90% sensitivity)
  • Patient does not carry HLA-B27 gene
  • Low pre-test probability for spondyloarthropathies

Action: Report as negative. Clinical symptoms should prompt alternative diagnostic workup if spondyloarthropathy is still suspected.

Plot 4: ABNORMAL HLA-B27 - POSITIVE RESULT

Clinical Context: Patient with inflammatory back pain and HLA-B27 positive

Isotype Control (Background)
HLA-B27 Test (POSITIVE)

⚠️ POSITIVE HLA-B27 RESULT - REQUIRES CLINICAL CORRELATION

Key Findings:

  • Isotype Control: Clean background (<5%)
  • HLA-B27 Test: BRIGHT population in upper-right quadrant (85%)
  • MFI: >5940 (clearly positive)
  • Clear separation: Test signal >> isotype control

Clinical Interpretation: POSITIVE — HLA-B27 ANTIGEN DETECTED

Important Notes:

  • Patient IS a carrier of the HLA-B27 gene
  • ~8% of HLA-B27 positive individuals develop ankylosing spondylitis
  • However, 90% sensitivity means a positive test SUGGESTS spondyloarthropathy
  • Result must be interpreted with clinical findings

Associated Diseases & Positivity Rates:

  • Ankylosing Spondylitis: ~90% (strong association)
  • Reactive Arthritis: 40-80%
  • Inflammatory Bowel Disease: 35-75%
  • Psoriatic Arthritis: 40-50%

Action: Report as POSITIVE. Physician should correlate with clinical presentation (inflammatory back pain, morning stiffness, imaging findings) to confirm or exclude ankylosing spondylitis.

Plot 5: HLA-B27 GREY ZONE (Equivocal Result)

Clinical Context: Borderline HLA-B27 signal, likely HLA-B7 interference

Isotype Control
HLA-B27 Test (GREY ZONE)

⚠️ GREY ZONE HLA-B27 - INCONCLUSIVE

Key Findings:

  • MFI: 4860-5940 (BORDERLINE range)
  • Signal Elevation: Slightly above isotype control but below clear positive
  • Cells in gate: ~25% (between negative <5% and positive >80%)
  • Likely Cause: HLA-B7 (PE-labeled antibody) interference or gene polymorphism (HLA-B27/B7 allele variation)

The Problem: The HLA-B27-FITC antibody may cross-react with HLA-B7, or the patient's HLA alleles may have intermediate expression patterns, making the result ambiguous.

Clinical Management:

  • DO NOT report as simply "positive" or "negative"
  • Document the result as "GREY ZONE - Inconclusive"
  • Recommend genetic HLA typing to determine actual HLA-B27 status
  • Consider repeating the flow test with fresh reagent
  • Advise clinician that definitive diagnosis requires genetic confirmation

Follow-up Action: Recommend HLA-B27 DNA sequencing or other immunological methods for definitive genotyping.
